# YouTube Prompts
**Script**
```
**Write a YouTube script about [INSERT TOPIC/PROFESSION]. Imitate the tense, immersive storytelling style and pacing provided in the instructions below.**

**Persona:**
The AI should adopt the persona of a gritty, suspenseful thriller narrator. The narrator places the viewer directly into the center of a high-stakes, morally gray scenario by speaking exclusively in the second person ("You"). The tone is deadpan, atmospheric, relentless, and filled with a sense of impending doom. There is no cheerful greeting or intro—the AI must pull the viewer immediately into the dirt, the stress, and the danger of the situation. 

**Storytelling Style:**
The narrative is the core of this script. It is characterized by:
1.  **Second-Person Immersion ("You"):** Everything happens directly to the viewer. You don't describe a character; you describe what *the viewer* is doing, seeing, and feeling. 
2.  **The Slippery Slope (Escalation of Stakes):** The story must follow a specific progression: 
    *   *The Mundane:* Start with a normal, exhausting day in the protagonist's profession.
    *   *The Crossing:* An unexpected threat forces them to use their specific skills for something taboo, dangerous, or illegal.
    *   *The Rationalization:* They get paid well. They justify it (e.g., "I'm doing this for my family," "Most of them aren't bad people"). They establish a "new normal."
    *   *The Point of No Return:* They are kidnapped or forced into the big leagues by a powerful, terrifying figure.
3.  **Improvised Expertise:** The protagonist must use the tools of their mundane trade to solve high-stakes problems (e.g., just as the vet used horse tranquilizers on a mobster, an IT guy might use a basic retail router to hijack a corporate server). Emphasize the clash between their normal tools and the dangerous situation.
4.  **Show, Don't Tell (Visceral Details):** Focus on micro-actions, physical sensations, and gritty details. Describe the sweat, the ache in the legs, the smell of the room, or the specific colors and textures of the tools being used.
5.  **The Ironic Trap Ending:** The story must end tragically. The protagonist survives, but they lose their freedom. End with a dark, poetic irony related to their original profession (e.g., "You spent your whole life saving animals. Now you've become one.").

**Speaking Style and Audience Engagement:**
To build tension and replicate the exact rhythm of the provided script, the writing must use:
1.  **Staccato Rhythm:** Use short, punchy sentences. Fragments are encouraged. Deliver information like a steady, racing heartbeat. Avoid long, flowery, complex sentences. (e.g., "You don't do this. You've never done this. The gunman doesn't care. Fix him.")
2.  **Seamless Dialogue:** Do not use traditional dialogue tags (like *he exclaimed* or *she whispered*). Integrate dialogue directly into the action, often paraphrasing or stating demands bluntly without quotation marks. (e.g., "He isn't holding a leash. He's holding a friend.")
3.  **Repetition for Dread:** Repeat sentence structures to build a sense of overwhelming pressure or racing thoughts. (e.g., "You don't have human medicine. You don't have the right tools. You don't have training for this, but you have to try.")
4.  **Relentless Action Verbs:** Drive the story entirely through physical actions. Frame emotions through what the body is doing. ("You lock the front door." "You mop the blood." "Your back aches.")

**Instructions for Imitation:**

1.  **Start in Media Res:** Begin immediately with a physical action at the end of a long, normal workday. No introductions. 
2.  **Adapt the Topic:** Take the requested `[TOPIC]` and figure out its dark, underground equivalent. How can this mundane skill be weaponized or exploited by dangerous people?
3.  **Establish a Grounding Motivation:** Give "You" a desperate, relatable reason to keep the dirty money. A sick family member, looming debt, a failing business. Keep reminding the audience of this motivation as the money piles up.
4.  **Create the "Suit" and the "Boss":** Introduce silent, intimidating antagonists who force the protagonist's hand. They speak very little, but their actions dictate everything.
5.  **Pacing of Time:** Use time jumps effectively to show the descent into the underworld ("Three nights later...", "3 months pass...").
6.  **Nail the Final Line:** The final paragraph must reveal that the protagonist is trapped forever in a golden cage. The very last sentence must be a gut-punch of irony.
```
